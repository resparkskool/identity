Beneath the Roles

A journey back to whoever you were before the job titles started.

Who Are You When No One's Asking What You Do?

Most of us answer the question "who are you?" with a job title, a relationship, a list of things we're responsible for. That's not dishonest. It's just incomplete. A role is something you do. It is not automatically the same thing as who you are — even when you've been doing it for twenty years.

Here's the thing this experience exists to prove to you: you are not empty beneath your roles. You're full. The roles are the costume. We're here to find whoever has been wearing it the whole time — and who doesn't disappear when the costume changes.

You are not broken. You are not lost. You are simply remembering.

This isn't a course and there's nothing to submit. It takes about twenty minutes, start to finish, and nothing is required of you once you're done. Move through the tabs above in order the first time — Your Roles → Role vs Identity → The Excavation → Your Anchor → Take It Further — each one builds on what you wrote in the last. After that, jump around however you want; everything you type stays right here in the page as you move between tabs.

Role vs Identity

Tap each role. Watch what's still standing underneath.

Picture your sense of self as an iceberg. The roles you listed are the part above the water — visible to everyone, including you. They're how you're introduced, what's on your resume, what changes when your circumstances change.

Below the surface is the much larger mass that's actually holding everything up: your core values, your natural personality, your innate gifts, the experiences that shaped you, the version of you that exists in total solitude with no audience at all.

Tap a role to submerge it — and notice you're still standing here.
Your roles are costumes you wear, not the actor beneath them.

Think of a time one of your major roles changed or ended completely — a job loss, a move, a relationship that ended, a kid who left for college. What part of you stayed exactly the same through it?
